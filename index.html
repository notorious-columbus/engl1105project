Privacy Policy, Explained Simply


ENGL 1105 Project by Aman Anwar

I used LLMs to summarise the privacy policies of many major tools and explained them lucidly in simple sentences. This projects aims to raise awareness about the encroaching privacy policies of many popular services we use everyday. Usually the privacy policies are mumbo jumbo, on purpose. This project has 9 of such popular websites and their ratings.


The privacy policies are rated on a scale of 1 to 5, where a higher number indicates a better privacy policy

Canva
2

Your personal data is not sold, however Canva may collect and share your location data. User generated content can be blocked or censored for no apparent reason. You are being fingerprinted, tracked by third party vendors.

Microsoft Office
1

Your personal data is not sold, however Microsoft may collect and share your location data. User generated content can be blocked or censored or removed for no apparent reason. You are being fingerprinted, tracked by third party vendors. You waive the right to a class action lawsuit.

Facebook
1

Your personal data is not sold, however Facebook may collect and share your location data, read you private messages. User generated content can be blocked or censored for no apparent reason. You are being fingerprinted, tracked by third party vendors. Deleted things are really not deleted.

Apple Services
2

Your personal data is shared with third parties involved with Apple Computer Inc. This service keeps a license on user-generated content even after users close their accounts. User generated content can be blocked or censored for no apparent reason. This service may use your personal information for marketing purposes.

Snapchat
1

You waive your moral rights. This service holds onto content that you've deleted.Snapchat collects and shares your location data. User generated content can be blocked or censored or removed for no apparent reason. You are being fingerprinted, tracked by third party vendors.

Wikipedia
5

Your personal data is not sold, however the service may use tracking pixels, web beacons, browser fingerprinting, and/or device fingerprinting on users. The service will resist legal requests for user information where reasonably possible. This service has easy to read terms.

Reddit
1

Your personal data is shared with third parties. This service keeps a license on user-generated content even after users close their accounts. User generated content can be blocked or censored for no apparent reason. This service uses your personal information for marketing purposes. You wavie off moral rights.

Khan Academy
